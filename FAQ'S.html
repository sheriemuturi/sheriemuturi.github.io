FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Questions and Answers

Here are some answers to questions you may have;

  1. What do I need when applying for a loan?

    • Fully completed loan application form.
    • Fully completed loan application form.
    • Guarantors, who must be active members and have contributed for at least six months. Guarantors’ and applicants shares MUST be equal to or more than the loan applied for.
    • Employer MUST sign and rubber stamp the application form.
    • Outstanding loan by the applicant MUST be cleared before a loan of the same kind is approved.
    • Two current pay slip certified by the applicant’s employer’s payroll officer MUST be attached to the application.
    • A copy of an acceptable form of identification (National ID/Valid Passport) MUST be attached to the application
    • Must have been an active member for at least six months.

  2. Must I have guarantors when applying for a loan? Can I guarantee my own loan?

      3. All loans granted by the Society must be fully guaranteed by members of the Society. Guarantors’ and applicants shares MUST be equal to or more than the loan applied for. In special cases, when loan is less/equal to shares one could self guarantee but have to state in writing and will need to be approved by full the Management Committee. Guarantor cannot be committee members, staff members & spouse.

  3. How much loan can I qualify for?

      4. Loans are given three times the member’s savings subject to a maximum of 2 million less any outstanding loan balance. So long as the two thirds rule is not violated (a member must take home at least a third of his/her basic pay after all deductions).The maximum amount of loan one can take is normally set by members at an AGM.

  4. Can I apply for more than one loan?

      5. A member is allowed to take all the loan products on offer so long as total loans granted do not exceed 2 million at any given time so long as all the other conditions are met currently. At any one time, a member must have only one normal loan and one emergency loan.

  5. Can I apply for a top up of an existing loan?

    • This product is currently available only on development and emergency loan
    • The loan being bridged must have run for 3 months
    • A member with an existing development loan is allowed to apply for a new bridging loan upon which the Society will recover the balance of the existing loan and give the member the difference subject to a maximum of Ksh 600, 000. Charges are 1.5% interest on balance and Ksh 500.00 processing fee.
    • Bridging is at least 3 months.

  6. When can I apply for a refinance loan?

      7. A member with an existing development loan or home development loan can apply for a refinance loan repayable in 54 months for development loan and 12 months for emergency.

  7. How often are development loans granted?

      8. Development loans are granted once a month. This enables the credit team to do all necessary follow-ups on the capacity and character of the borrower.

  8. What loan products does the Society have?

    9. NORMAL DEVELOPMENT LOAN

    • Normal development loans are considered once a month. The loan application should be received before the 15th day of every month. Credit Committee meets on or before 15th day of every month to consider the applications
    • Maximum repayment period is fifty four (54) months
    • Loan granted is three times the member’s savings less all outstanding loans provided that the one third rule is not violated. The maximum amount is Kshs 2,000,000.
    • Granted once a month

    HOME APPLIANCES

    • Home appliances loans are considered once a month. The loan application should be received before the 15th of every month. Credit Committee meets before 15th of every month to consider the applications
    • Maximum repayment period is twelve (12) months PLUS 5% fee
    • Loan granted is value of item identified taking into account the member’s savings less all outstanding loans provided that one third rule is not violated. The maximum loan granted shall be Ksh 50 000 less the insurance premium.

    REFINANCING LOAN

    • A member must have a special development and emergency or a development loan to qualify for a refinancing loan.
    • Maximum Ksh 600,000 is granted provided the two-third rule is not violated
    • Maximum repayment period is 54 months and the amount granted is three times a member’s shares less all outstanding loans
    • Granted once a month (depending on the society’s liquidity)

    EMERGENCY

    Emergencies include sudden hospitalization, funeral expenses, court fines and other unforeseen circumstances beyond the member’s control, but shall not include house rent:

    • Maximum amount is Ksh 50 000 each.
    • Maximum repayment period is twelve months
    • Granted as applications are received. Applications should be forwarded to CC same day such that the member gets the cheque after maximum 2 days.

    SCHOOL FEES LOANS

    • No maximum but must have ability to repay
    • Processed as the applications are received
    • Repayment within the calendar years
    • Requires one to attach fees structure
    • Cheques written to schools

    COLLEGE FEE LOANS

    • As school fees but repayable within 24 months

FOR MEMBER’S INFORMATION

  1. Members Benefits

    • Sacco interest rates on both savings and loans are generally better than those offered by commercial banks.
    • Members get educated in financial matters by teaching prudent handling, budgeting and keep track of money
    • Savings provide security for future financial needs.
    • Savings accumulates funds for use now and after retirement
    • High savings will qualify for higher loans
    • High savings need less guarantors
    • High savings will result in high dividends
    • High savings will strengthen the society’s lending capacity
    • Savings will act as an assurance in case of death whereby the nominee is paid twice the accumulated shares and outstanding
    • High savings guarantees a member a comfortable retirement.

  2. Rights of Members

    • No maximum but must have ability to repay
    • Processed as the applications are received
    • Repayment within the calendar years
    • Requires one to attach fees structure
    • Cheques written to schools

  3. What are the responsibilities (obligations) of a member?

    • No maximum but must have ability to repay
    • Processed as the applications are received
    • Repayment within the calendar years
    • Requires one to attach fees structure
    • Cheques written to schools
