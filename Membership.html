Membership

HOW TO BE A MEMBER

  1. Complete application form in triplicate with mandatory entrance fee of Ksh.200
  2. Pay the entrance fee;
  3. Submit the form to Management Board for consideration;
  4. The member to fill the authority to deduct the money through check off

Member's Rights

  • To be admitted upon application and be issued with membership card. If a member’s application is rejected, He/She shall be advised within 2 days.
  • To apply for any of the loans offered by the SACCO as per LOAN POLICY in place.
  • To have their loan application acknowledged and outcome communicated within 24 hours after consideration.
  • To have their emergency loans considered and outcome communicated within 2 days.
  • To have their loans given on first-come first-served-basis.
  • To guarantee and be guaranteed for a loan.
  • To attend GMs, vote and contest any elective office if eligible, receive dividends and interest on savings.
  • If funds are erroneously recovered, the member is entitled to a refund with 2 days of formal notification.
  • Members may make complaints /suggestions in whatever form.
  • Beneficiary/Nominees are entitled to receive your deposits(savings) upon death. Such payment will be made within a month upon receipt of all supportive documents.
  • To withdraw from the SACCO in accordance to the By-laws.

    • Member's Obligations

    • Obey all laws of the land.
    • Members are obliged to provide timely, accurate and authentic documents to facilitate provision of services..
    • Members are obliged to be courteous and respectful to all UBORA committee members and staff.
    • Members are obliged to acquaint themselves with the Society’s By-laws, the Co-operatives Societies Act, the Society Rules, GM resolutions and other legal instruments which govern the SACCO movement.
    • Members are expected to be of good character, with co-operative spirit and give feed back on the services being rendered by the UBORA SACCO.
    • It is your obligation as a member to help the SACCO combat corruption by not offering inducements by way of gifts and offers to committee and staff members or to solicit the same in return of services. You’re also obliged to report all cases of corruption to the relevant authorities.
